Scoring System & Criteria Weights

Understanding the mathematical foundation of our rankings. Weights are strictly allocated based on survival in an AI-driven economy.

Strategic Prioritization

The technology landscape is shifting rapidly. Empirical data shows routine programming and entry-level positions are facing unprecedented automation. Our scoring model heavily prioritizes careers that either build the automation or require profound physical-world context.

High Impact Weights (x3): AI Resistance, Market Demand Growth, and Longevity are given maximum weight. A high salary is irrelevant if the role will be automated or obsolete within a decade.

Medium Impact (x2): Compensation, Complexity, Innovation, and Competition levels provide the core assessment of the day-to-day career quality.

Methodology & Formulas

How we transform structured logic and domain research into an objective, mathematical ranking.

The 10 Core Criteria Definitions

AI Resistance
Ability to withstand automation by LLMs and autonomous coding agents.
Demand Growth
Projected global hiring volume increase based on industry trajectories.
Earning Potential
Global salary ceilings and compensation scaling.
Barrier to Entry
Difficulty of acquiring required skills (protects against market saturation).
Complexity
Depth of architectural or systemic problem-solving required.
Global Opportunity
Ability to work remotely or cross-border without local restrictions.
Innovation Potential
Proximity to cutting-edge research and new tech paradigms.
Longevity
Expected lifespan of the core technologies before obsolescence.
Competition Level
Volume of graduates competing for the same roles. (Reverse scored).
Learning ROI
Financial and career return relative to the educational time invested.

Calculation Formulas

Each role is scored from 0 to 10 across all criteria. To calculate the final rankings, we apply two mathematical formulas. The Adjusted Score uses exponential weighting to severely punish roles that fail in critical areas like AI Resistance.

Base Final Score:
Score = SUM(value × weight) / SUM(weights)
Adjusted Score (Exponential Weighting):
Adj = SUM(value × weight^1.5) / SUM(weight^1.5)

Scoring Transparency & Limitations

While our methodology is rigorous and data-driven, macroeconomic forecasts inherently involve probability rather than absolute certainty. Understanding these constraints is vital for accurate career planning.

  • Scores represent research-based estimates (0-10 scale) aggregated from multiple global public sources.
  • Individual role scores may vary ±0.5 points based on specific regional market conditions and sector.
  • 15-year projections are probabilistic assessments of current tech trajectories, subject to sudden breakthrough disruptions.
  • Competition Level is reverse-scored: higher values indicate lower candidate saturation (better for the applicant).
  • Exponential weighting intentionally amplifies penalties for roles with low AI Resistance or short Longevity.
  • Triangulate these scores with personal aptitude and continuous skill adaptation planning.
